The “Anti-Silicon Valley” Pitch

📈 Typical VC Pitch

  • Hyper-growth at all costs
  • Salesy, disruption language
  • Hockey-stick financial projections
  • Winner-take-all market framing
  • Success = exit or IPO

🌟 Social Venture Pitch

  • Systems change over time
  • Empathy and community trust
  • Credibility through fieldwork
  • Impact metrics, not vanity metrics
  • Success = lives changed

You are not pitching a product. You are making a credible case that you understand a problem deeply enough to solve it — and that you can be trusted with resources to try.

🎥  The Format: 5-Minute Pairwise Video

Recorded YouTube video — judged head-to-head in a pairwise comparison

⏲ The Constraint

  • Exactly 5 minutes — no more
  • Recorded to YouTube (unlisted or public)
  • Judges see two pitches side-by-side and pick the clearer one
  • No live Q&A — the video must stand alone

⚠️ Why Cognitive Ease Wins

  • Judges are not experts in your field
  • Confusion is disqualifying, not forgivable
  • If the judge spends 3 minutes trying to figure out what your venture actually does, you lose
  • Clarity is a form of respect for your audience

💡 The winning pitch is not the most complex one. It is the most legible one.

The Social Pitch Structure

1

The Human Problem

Ground the audience in a specific, felt human experience — not just a statistic. Make them care before you make them think.

2

The Fieldwork Insight

Share what you uniquely learned by being in the field — the non-obvious truth that others have missed.

3

The Solution

Explain what you actually do — in plain language. No jargon. One or two concrete sentences.

4

The Impact

Define success in measurable terms. What changes in the world if you succeed?

5

Call to Action

Tell the audience exactly what you need from them — funding, partnership, feedback, or belief.

Problem vs. Insight

🔴 The Problem

The macro-level issue your venture addresses — the condition that already exists in the world, documentable by data.

"Food insecurity affects 1 in 6 children in Hamilton County."

Anyone can cite this statistic. It does not yet explain why you.

💡 The Fieldwork Insight

What you uniquely discovered by being present in the community — the non-obvious truth that reshapes how the problem is understood.

"It is actually about time-poverty. Parents skip meals to save time, not money — a discovery that only emerged from sitting in kitchens with families."

The Insight is your competitive advantage. It is what justifies your solution and separates you from every organization that also “addresses food insecurity.”

🤔  “What Do You Actually Do?”

❌ Jargon-Heavy

“We leverage synergistic paradigms to co-create scalable, human-centered solutions at the intersection of food systems and community capacity-building.”

The judge has no idea what you do. They are now skeptical of everything else you say. You have lost.

✅ Plain Language

“We deliver pre-chopped, 15-minute meal kits to working parents in the West End, priced at $2 per bag and subsidized by hospital partners.”

Who, what, where, how much — all in one sentence. The judge immediately understands your model and is ready to evaluate it.

The test: Can someone who has never heard of your venture explain it back to you in one sentence? If not, rewrite.

Part 2

Adapting the Story

The same venture, told three different ways — because the same words do not land the same way with every audience.

The Three Personas

📋

Skeptical Foundation Officer

Persona 1 — The Evaluator
Cares about
  • Logic, evidence, and rigor
  • Impact metrics and measurability
  • Replicability and scale potential
  • Responsible, accountable use of funds
🤝

Target Beneficiary

Persona 2 — The Neighbor
Cares about
  • Trust and lived experience
  • Whether this is actually for them
  • Utility: does it solve a real problem?
  • Respect and dignity in the framing
🏢

Corporate Partner

Persona 3 — The Brand
Cares about
  • Brand alignment and visibility
  • ESG commitments and PR value
  • Low reputational risk
  • Employee engagement opportunities

🤖  The AI Persona Prompt

Copy this prompt, paste your core story, and generate three tailored drafts.

📋 AI Prompt — Persona Rewrite

I am pitching my social venture. Here is my core story: [PASTE 1-2 PARAGRAPHS DESCRIBING YOUR VENTURE HERE] Please rewrite this story three different times, tailored to three distinct audiences: 1. A skeptical foundation program officer who cares about logic and metrics. 2. A target beneficiary who cares about trust and utility. 3. A potential corporate partner who cares about brand alignment.

✍️  Work Time

Use the AI prompt to generate three persona drafts — then read them critically.

20:00

💡 While the timer runs, ask yourself:

  • Which persona draft is most persuasive — and why?
  • What did the AI change between versions?
  • Where does the AI sound generic rather than authentic?
  • What specific detail could only you add?

The Human Value-Add

✏️ Your Task: Critique and Rewrite

The AI has given you three competent drafts. Now your job is to make one of them actually yours.

1

Pick ONE persona — the audience that matters most to your venture right now

Rewrite the AI draft to add three things the AI cannot supply:

📍 Specific Hamilton Anecdotes

A real street, conversation, or moment from your fieldwork that no AI could invent

🌟 Authentic Phrasing

Language that sounds like you — not like a press release or a grant template

🧠 Human Nuance

Complexity or uncertainty that builds credibility rather than hiding it

🗣  Share Out

Discussion Question

"How did your story shift when you moved from pitching to a funder versus pitching to a beneficiary? What changed — and what had to stay the same?"

What typically changes

  • ›Tone (authoritative vs. empathetic)
  • ›Evidence type (data vs. story)
  • ›What success looks like to them

What must stay the same

  • ›The core facts and truthfulness
  • ›Your values and mission framing
  • ›Respect for the community you serve
