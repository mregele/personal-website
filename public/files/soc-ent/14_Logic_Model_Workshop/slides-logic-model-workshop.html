Part 1

Quick Refresher

The Logic Model Chain

Your venture's theory of how change happens

💰 Inputs

Resources you invest

⚙ Activities

What you do

📊 Outputs

Direct products

🎯 Outcomes

Changes that result

🌟 Impact

Long-term change

Example: Mobile Health Clinic Venture

Funding, nurses, mobile clinic vehicle, medical supplies

Mobile clinics visit rural areas; administer vaccines & screenings

500 patients seen per month; 1,200 vaccinations given

Increased vaccination rates; earlier disease detection

Reduced preventable illness & mortality in underserved communities

⚠ The #1 Mistake

Confusing Outputs with Outcomes

✘ Outputs (Not Change)

  • "50 workshops delivered"
  • "200 people attended training"
  • "1,000 meals served"
  • "15 businesses counseled"

These count what you did, not whether it worked

vs

✔ Outcomes (Real Change)

  • "Participants gained new skills"
  • "Income increased by 20%"
  • "Food insecurity reduced"
  • "Businesses increased revenue"

These measure actual change in people's lives

Outcomes Have a Timeline

Short-Term

Immediate changes
(skills, knowledge, attitudes)

→

Mid-Term

Behavioral changes
(actions, practices)

→

Long-Term Impact

Systemic change
(community, population)

Example: Training → Skills gained (short) → Better job performance (mid) → Career advancement & income growth (long)

Part 2

AI Stress-Test

🤖 Why Use AI as a Critic?

"It's easy to overlook leaps of faith in our own logic. We're going to use an AI assistant as a critical 'friendly enemy' to stress-test our models and expose the hidden assumptions we all make."

🤖 The Stress-Test Prompt

I am a social entrepreneur developing a Logic Model for my venture. Here is my draft: Inputs: [List your inputs] Activities: [List your activities] Outputs: [List your outputs] Outcomes (Short-term): [List short-term outcomes] Outcomes (Mid-term): [List mid-term outcomes] Long-Term Impact: [State your impact] Act as a skeptical and rigorous impact evaluation consultant (like from the Gates Foundation or a major federal grantmaker). Your job is to find the weaknesses in my causal chain. Please identify: 1. The single weakest causal link in my model (the jump from an activity to an outcome that seems least plausible). Explain why it is weak. 2. One major hidden assumption I am making that, if false, would cause the entire model to fail. 3. One potential negative unintended consequence that could result from my activities, even if successful.

💡 Your Task: Act on the Critique

The AI finds problems. You fix them.

🧐 1. Analyze

Do you agree with the AI's assessment of the weakest link and hidden assumption?

  • Why or why not?
  • What context is the AI missing?

💪 2. Strengthen

How can you strengthen the identified weak link?

  • Add a new activity?
  • Add an intermediate outcome?
  • Change the logic?

🛡 3. Mitigate

How could you monitor for or mitigate the negative consequence?

  • What would you watch for?
  • How would you respond?

💻 Work Time: AI Stress-Test

Instructions

  1. Open your draft Logic Model from Feb 17
  2. Copy the prompt and paste it into ChatGPT or Claude
  3. Fill in your Logic Model components
  4. Read the AI's critique carefully
  5. Document: What's the weakest link? The hidden assumption? The risk?
  6. Draft your revisions to address each issue

Part 3

Peer Review

👥 Peer Review Process

💬 Share (5 min each)

  • Your original Logic Model
  • The AI's critique (weakest link, assumption, risk)
  • Your proposed revisions

Partner Feedback Focus

Does the revision successfully address the weak link?
Is the plan to validate the hidden assumption realistic?
Is the mitigation plan for negative consequences practical?
What's still the biggest risk in the model?

👥 Peer Review & Revision Time

Round 1: Partner A Shares

Share your model, the AI critique, and your revisions. Partner B provides feedback.

Round 2: Partner B Shares

Switch roles. Partner B shares, Partner A provides feedback.

25:00

Final 10 minutes: Individual revision time to strengthen your Logic Model

Part 4

Debrief

💬 Large Group Discussion

"What was the most common type of 'weak link' the AI found in our models?"

"What hidden assumptions kept appearing across different ventures?"

"What surprised you about the AI's critique of your model?"

"What does this teach us about designing for impact?"

💡 The Big Takeaway

The assumptions you identified today are exactly what you need to test through field research. Your Logic Model tells you where to focus your interviews and observations.

Logic Models → Field Research

  • Weak links tell you what causal claims need evidence
  • Hidden assumptions become interview questions
  • Potential risks become things to watch for in observation
  • Your model is a living document - revise as you learn
