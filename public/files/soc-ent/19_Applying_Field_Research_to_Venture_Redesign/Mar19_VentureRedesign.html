Learning Objectives

  • Connect field insights to strategic decisions — understand how a single disconfirming observation can justify a major redesign
  • Utilize AI for divergent brainstorming — generate multiple pivot options from one piece of evidence before evaluating any of them

  • Distinguish between minor refinements and pivots that require rethinking core assumptions of your venture model

The Pivot, Defined

“A change in strategy without a change in vision.”

— Eric Ries, The Lean Startup

Vision (Stays the Same)
The impact you seek. The community you serve. The world you want to create.
Strategy (Changes)
How you deliver. Who you target. What problem you solve. How you sustain it.

The Pivot Mindset

The difference between founders who grow and those who stall is rarely intelligence — it’s this.

❌ Confirmation Mode

  • Seeks data that confirms the original plan
  • Treats disconfirming evidence as noise
  • “Our users just don’t understand it yet”
  • Ego is tied to the original idea
  • Pivoting feels like failure

✅ Curiosity Mode

  • Seeks data that challenges the original plan
  • Treats disconfirming evidence as a gift
  • “Our users are showing us something important”
  • Ego is tied to the mission, not the method
  • Pivoting feels like learning

💡 Curiosity over confirmation. Evidence over ego.

Five Types of Pivots

Change strategy, not vision — one dimension at a time.

Pivot Name Definition Real Example
Customer
Segment
Same solution, different user. A different population turns out to benefit most — not the group you originally designed for.
🎬 YouTube: Video-dating site for singles
↓
Global video-sharing platform for everyone
Problem
Same users, different problem. The real pain is upstream, downstream, or adjacent to what you assumed.
🎮 Slack: Internal chat tool for a game studio
↓
Communication platform for all businesses
Solution
Same problem, different mechanism. A radically different approach delivers the outcome better — often for a completely different context.
🧹 Play-Doh: Soapy wallpaper-cleaning putty
↓
Non-toxic modeling clay for children
Channel
Same solution, different distribution. How you reach, deliver to, or support users changes entirely.
💿 Netflix: Physical DVD-by-mail service
↓
Instant digital streaming on any device
Revenue
Model
Same value proposition, different monetization. Who pays, how much, and in what form all change.
💻 Adobe: One-time $700 software license
↓
Creative Cloud: $50/month subscription

Part 1

The Reality Check

⚡ Sharing Your Catalyst

From your Mar 17 synthesis: the disconfirming evidence that “set a box in your Logic Model on fire.”

👥 Partner Share-Out — 8 minutes

1

📌 Identify Your Catalyst

State the specific fieldwork quote or observation — the exact moment that unsettled your assumptions. Be concrete: not “they didn’t engage” but what precisely happened, where, and who said it.

2

🔥 Which Box Burned?

Point to the specific Logic Model box that this evidence directly breaks. Name it precisely: “My Activities assumption that weekly workshops would build community trust” — not just “the middle part.”

3

🧲 Strategic Implication

In one sentence: what does this evidence force you to fundamentally rethink? Not “we should tweak our messaging” — but “we must reconsider [core assumption] because the data shows [what].”

🏆 This three-part structure is the backbone of your Exit Ticket: My Venture Pivot — due tonight at 11:59 PM.

Refinement vs. Pivot

Not all responses to disconfirming evidence require the same magnitude of change.

Minor Refinement Strategic Adjustment Full Pivot
Tweak
Expand
Shift
Reinvent
🟢 Refinement

Vision and core strategy unchanged. You adjust messaging, timing, or surface-level execution based on feedback.

e.g., “Users want weekly sessions, not monthly”

🟠 Strategic Adjustment

Vision intact, one strategic variable changes significantly. You re-target, re-scope, or re-route — but the core logic holds.

e.g., “We should partner with schools, not reach families directly”

🔴 Full Pivot

Evidence demands a fundamental rethinking. A core assumption about the user, problem, or solution model is broken and must be replaced.

e.g., “The people we designed for can’t use this at all — but a different group is already adapting it themselves”

🔎 Ask: How many Logic Model boxes does my disconfirming evidence invalidate? One box = refinement. Three or more = pivot territory.

Part 2

Divergent Brainstorming

🤖 AI as Your Brainstorming Consultant

“A single insight can lead to multiple pivot paths. We use AI to think expansively — generating every plausible direction before our judgment narrows the field.”

📈 Why AI is Good at This

  • No ego tied to your original idea
  • Generates options without self-censoring
  • Connects across industries and contexts
  • Speed: 5 pivot options in 30 seconds

🚫 What AI Cannot Do

  • Know your community’s specific context
  • Judge what is truly mission-aligned
  • Weigh relationships and trust built
  • Make the final strategic call for you

🎯 The Right Division of Labor

  • AI: generate 5 divergent options
  • You: critically evaluate each one
  • Evidence: your filter for viability
  • Mission: your filter for alignment

📋 The AI Redesign Prompt

Copy this prompt → fill in your details → paste into Claude or ChatGPT

You are a Creative Social Innovation Consultant with deep expertise in social entrepreneurship, pivot strategy, and impact design. Your role is to help a student social entrepreneur think expansively — generating bold, diverse strategic options before any evaluation takes place. My original venture concept: [DESCRIBE YOUR CONCEPT — target population, core problem, proposed solution] My core vision (what impact I ultimately seek): [DESCRIBE YOUR MISSION IN 1-2 SENTENCES] The most important disconfirming finding from my field research: [PASTE YOUR CATALYST QUOTE OR KEY OBSERVATION — be specific and direct] The Logic Model assumption this evidence breaks: [WHICH BOX — e.g., "My Activities assumption that weekly workshops would build skills"] Using only the evidence above, generate FIVE distinct pivot directions for my venture. For each option: 1. Name the pivot TYPE (Customer Segment / Problem / Solution / Channel / Revenue Model) 2. Describe the new strategic direction in 2-3 sentences 3. Explain what stays the same (vision) and what changes (strategy) 4. Identify the single biggest risk of this pivot path 5. Rate the magnitude: Minor Adjustment (1) → Major Reinvention (5) Include at least one "wild card" option — something counterintuitive or uncomfortable. Do NOT filter for feasibility. We will evaluate viability ourselves. Generate options only.

⏲ Work Time

Run the AI Redesign Prompt. Read all five options. Don’t evaluate yet — just generate.

📋 Step 1 (5 min)

Fill in the Redesign Prompt with your venture’s details and your catalyst finding. Copy and paste into your AI tool.

🤖 Step 2 (10 min)

Read all five pivot options. Highlight anything that surprises you or makes you uncomfortable — those are the interesting ones.

📝 Step 3 (10 min)

For each option, write one sentence: What would need to be true for this to work? Don’t kill options yet — just note conditions.

🌟 Step 4 (before debrief)

Star your top 2 options. You will defend your final choice in the Share-Out.

The Human Value-Add

AI generated your options. Now you do what AI cannot: critique them with context, values, and judgment.

📋 Filter 1: Viability

Does this pivot require resources, relationships, or capabilities you don’t have? What’s the realistic path to make it work?

  • What partnerships would I need?
  • What skills does my team lack?
  • What’s the minimum viable version?

❤️ Filter 2: Mission Alignment

Does this direction still serve the people your venture exists for? Or does it drift toward impact-washing?

  • Whose voice is centered here?
  • Does this create real equity impact?
  • Would your original community endorse it?

📈 Filter 3: Evidence Fit

Does the pivot actually respond to what your field evidence showed? Or is it an interesting idea that isn’t grounded in what you learned?

  • Which quotes support this direction?
  • What evidence would you still need?
  • Does it address the root cause?

💡 A pivot that passes all three filters — viable, mission-aligned, and evidence-grounded — is a strategic direction worth defending. That’s what your Exit Ticket: My Venture Pivot must demonstrate.

Justifying the Path

In your Exit Ticket: My Venture Pivot, you don’t just name a pivot — you justify it. A strategic argument has three parts.

📌 1 — The Evidence

State the specific catalyst: the quote, observation, or data point that makes the status quo untenable. Be direct and concrete. This is your foundation.

🤔 2 — The Logic

Explain why this evidence points to this specific pivot type. What does it reveal about your original assumption? What would happen if you didn’t pivot?

🎯 3 — The Strategic Direction

Articulate the new direction with precision: who the user is, what problem you’re solving, how you deliver it, and why this honors your original mission. This is the “so what” of all your fieldwork.
